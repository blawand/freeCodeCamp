Background

IU, whose real name is Lee Ji-eun, was born on May 16, 1993, in Seoul, South Korea. She faced financial difficulties in her early life, which led her to pursue a career in entertainment as a means to support her family. IU debuted as a singer at the age of fifteen with her album "Lost and Found" in 2008 and quickly became recognized for her vocal ability and genuine musical talent.

Music

IU has established herself as one of South Korea's most respected solo artists, known for her wide vocal range and emotive songwriting. She has received critical acclaim for albums like "Modern Times" and "Palette," which blend genres such as jazz, R&B, and pop while exploring themes of youth and self-discovery. Her chart-topping hits, including "Good Day," "Twenty-Three," and "Blueming," showcase her unique ability to connect with a broad audience across different musical styles.

Film

IU has also made significant strides in her acting career, starring in popular Korean dramas such as "Dream High," "Moon Lovers: Scarlet Heart Ryeo," and "My Mister," the latter earning her praise for her portrayal of complex characters. She made her big-screen debut in the 2019 film "Shades of the Heart," where she played the role of a woman who is reflecting on her relationships. IU's natural acting style and ability to convey deep emotions have garnered her a reputable standing in the film industry.
